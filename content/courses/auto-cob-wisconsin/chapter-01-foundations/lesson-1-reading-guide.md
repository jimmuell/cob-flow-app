# Lesson 1 — Reading Guide: Foundations

Read these sources in order. The reading guide sequences existing primary material; the authored teaching that synthesizes it lives in `lesson-2-summary.md`.

## Reading order

### 1. The vocabulary of COB (~20 min)

Read **ForwardHealth Master Deck** slides 1–7 (`../../../../../cob-auto-claim/reference/training/ForwardHealth_COB_Training_final.pdf`).

These slides cover: the COB definition, third-party liability (TPL), commercial health insurance, payer of last resort, provider-based billing. Wisconsin Medicaid's vocabulary maps closely to commercial COB terms; learning it here means recognizing it everywhere else.

### 2. The COB Flow project framing (~30 min)

Read **Product Spec § 1** (project overview) and **§ 5** (operational workflow) from `../../../../docs/COB_Flow_Product_Spec_v0.8.docx`.

Section 1 establishes why the product exists; Section 5 walks the operational workflow an analyst follows. Read these to anchor the rest of the course in actual analyst work, not abstract COB theory.

### 3. The recovery universe (~20 min)

Read **Handoff § 1** (project background) and **§ 4** (working norms) from `../../../../docs/COB_Flow_Handoff.md`.

> **Analyst track and deeper:** Section 1 of the Handoff includes the original strategic positioning — useful context for understanding where this work sits in the broader subrogation industry (Rawlings, Equian/Optum, Phia Group).

### 4. Wisconsin orientation (~15 min)

Read the **first three pages** of `../../../../../cob-auto-claim/reference/regulations/Wisconsin Legislature_ Ins 3.40(11)(a).pdf`.

Don't try to read it cover to cover yet — the full rule is the subject of Chapter 5. For now, look at the structure: who the rule covers, what "complying plan" vs "noncomplying plan" means, how it relates to the NAIC model. Recognition over comprehension.

### 5. Optional extension (Self track) (~30 min)

> **Self track only:** Skim `../../../../../cob-auto-claim/reference/handbooks/cob-smart-webinar-CBC-final-072116.pdf`. This is an industry-side view (CBC = COB Business Conference, the trade association). It surfaces vocabulary you'll hear in industry but rarely see in regulatory text.

## Total reading time

- Customer / Analyst track: ~85 minutes
- Self track with extension: ~115 minutes

## What you should be able to do after this lesson

- Define COB in your own words (test against `lesson-2-summary.md`)
- Distinguish COB from coordination of care (CoC), which sounds similar but lives in a different domain
- Recognize the four payer types likely to overlap in an Auto COB case: health plan, auto med-pay/PIP, at-fault liability, Medicare/Medicaid
- Articulate what "primacy" means at a sentence level

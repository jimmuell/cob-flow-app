## What "primacy" means

<!-- TO BE AUTHORED — Jim
Target length: 150–250 words.
Cover: primacy = which payer is legally responsible to pay first; secondary may then reduce its payment to keep total payments under 100% of allowable expenses.
Distinguish primacy from: contract coverage (does the policy cover this?), benefit limits (how much does it cover?), and clinical appropriateness (is this medically necessary?). Primacy is purely about ORDERING, not the other questions.

## Where Auto COB sits in the broader recovery universe

<!-- TO BE AUTHORED — Jim
Target length: 200–300 words.
Cover: the relationship between COB → subrogation → recovery. Where each begins and ends. COB orders payers; subrogation pursues third parties after a payment has been made; recovery is the overall pursuit. Auto COB sits at the intersection of all three: when an accident creates the multi-payer scenario, COB orders them, then if the plan paid more than it should have, subrogation/recovery pursues the at-fault driver to get the plan made whole.

## Terminology pitfall to settle now

**Coordination of Care** (clinical workflow between providers) is not **Coordination of Benefits** (financial workflow between payers). They share a name and live in different domains. Always say "COB" or "coordination of benefits" — never the unqualified "coordination."

## What you should be able to do after this lesson

- Define COB in your own words
- Distinguish COB from coordination of care, contract coverage, benefit limits, and clinical appropriateness
- Identify the four payer types likely to overlap in an Auto COB case
- Articulate primacy at a sentence level and identify what happens to secondary's payment math
- Recognize an Auto COB case at intake (Analyst track)
